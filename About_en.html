About me

Dan Lundberg, 50-year-old Kotka family father. With 11- and 13-year-old child everyday is quite speedy and the old house also takes care of the fact that liiemmin free time is left. If it remains, it will preferably go outdoors, reading or music. Chess play is a bit behind because there is no player to resist.

Music has always been a central part of life and especially older popular music as well American root music. Different forms of root music include: old Jazz, Western Swing, Hillbilly, Bluegrass, Cajun, Blues, Rhythm & Blues, Doo-Wop, Rock'n'Roll, Rockabilly, Country & Western, etc. Not only listening to music and recording Collecting also making a phone call is a part of your lifestyle. Musical instruments include guitar, mandolin and double bass.

From a young age, it has been an honor to be able to share the joys and sorrows with the people. Then there was a spark to help others and solve problems. Now being a psychotherapist is a continuation of this desirable desire. Actually solving difficult situations and puzzles is empowering and more dear a hobby like a bread job.

As a young man, the road took me to study shipping but, due to poor work conditions, getting a job at a hospital felt natural. Initially, some time went by in the care of the elderly until army time cut off working life. Work with the elderly continued after the army and was followed by an application to study a registered nurse. Specializing in Internal Medicine Surgical Nursing was actually an injury. Even during my studies, working with psychiatry seemed like the best thing to do. Many different hospitals and departments were visited during and after graduation. However, most with acute psychiatry. The long employment in the crisis unit and the specialization in psychiatric care was the moment when woke up to the dream of studying to become a psychotherapist sometime.

On the other hand, shipping that has been a tradition for centuries, the seas also demanded a revision of that road. Years passed in the field of acute psychiatry, with most of the closed wards until the urge to try ship work also grew so large that it led to further maritime studies. The officer's degree came and I did a lot of work at the same time; on ships and hospitals. After graduation, she spent many years in ship work. First on deck, then later as a passenger ship nurse and occupational health nurse. This work was greatly assisted by my experience in psychiatric nursing.

Along with the job, there was an opportunity to study as a psychotherapist. It really came to a good point. Previously, there had been the idea that it would be closer to retirement age with the goal of making it. In 2009, the history of DanCan began. Boat work made it possible for part-time psychotherapy, thanks to the long vacations work guidance was easy to do in a reasonable amount. The aim is to start a full-time job in psychotherapy, counseling and education.

During my working life I have done a lot of work therapy and met with such individuals, couples, entire families as well as work communities. In the mid-1990s, getting to know solution-oriented methods of help opened up one's own idea problem solving and gave a whole new perspective on how to handle situations that were seemed particularly challenging when approached with more traditional frameworks.

Today I have my own main goal in client work, that of getting help from the client. When he leaves the reception, I want him to have a new perspective on his problem, something concrete and how to go about it. In addition, it is important that he feels relieved. The actual purpose of the care is always created in cooperation with the client and it is the care the most important thing.
